Welcome!

Salvador is about art, fun, hands-on projects, meeting friends. It's about discovering new solutions, ideas and different ways to express our imagination and creativity in a safe and engaging place, while learning valuable skills. We believe that there are no mistakes in art and we value creativity and self-expression. Come and give us a visit!

Camps

Camps provide the time for students to use their hands and heads to explore creativity and make art! Children’s Art Studio "Salvador" invites your child to join us for special full-day camp sessions, where we dive deep into drawing, painting, sculpture, printing, and collage with elaborate large-scale projects that all students contribute to.
Each day we come together to draw, paint, sculpt and read from beautifully illustrated books that explore the history of art and art-making. Our classes are small and lead by two teachers. All students receive one-on-one instruction and thoughtful activities in a quiet, calm productive environment. Camps are for children between 5 and 10 years old.

Workshops

One-day workshops meet from 9am until 2pm throughout the year, including on days off from school. Workshops are for children ages 5 to 12. Workshops are a great time to step out of the rhythm of the school year and explore new dimensions of personal creativity.

What is a workshops like?

Children enter the studio and gravitate to a table covered with beautiful books and art materials. They may read or begin to work with the materials such as tape, string, crayons, markers, watercolor, wood, cardboard, and more. After the opportunity to adjust and explore, we sit down together on the floor to read and discuss the morning project. The morning project usually involves specific instruction on an art-making technique with the objective to learn to draw in a particular way, learn how to use an ink brush, or acquire an approach to making prints for example. After the morning project, we break for lunch and outdoor play. After outdoor play, we start a second project which might explore three-dimensional sculpture or a collaborative group project. At the end of the workshop we slow down and engage in group discussion to reflect on the work we created.
